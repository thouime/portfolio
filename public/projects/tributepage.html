Gautama Buddha

The History of Buddha.

Buddha Origins

Regarded as the founder, Gautama Buddha, is regarded as the founder of Buddhism, seen as a person that achieved freedom through meditation.

6th to 5th century BCE or 5th to 4th century BCE

From an Aristocratic Family

The Shakya clan that Buddha was born into was an aristocratic family among a tribe of rice farmers.

563–483 BCE

Spiritual Beginnings

Contemplating old age and death, Buddha sought liberation from these types of suffering.

563–483 BCE

Early Yoga Experiences

Gautama began practicing under two teachers using the physical, mental, and spiritual form of Yogic meditation. Not being satisfied he moved on to other practices.

533-528 BCE

Awakening

Learning that dhyana was the way to achieve awakening, Gautama entered into the practice as a way to moderate oneself away from severe self-indulgence.

533-528 BCE

Teaching Dharma

Originally, Buddha hesitated in teaching Dharma as many people are filled with the feelings of hate and ignorance. The path of enlightenment would be very difficult to grasp. Brahma Sahmpati eventually convinced him and the Buddha began teaching.

June or July 528 BCE

Saṅgha and its growth

The saṅgha, the company of Buddhits monks, traveled teaching a wide variety of people including even cannibals and murderers. Continuing to grow, the Buddha's saṅgha converted thousands of different people into their teachings including much of Buddah's family members.

528-527 BCE

The Pratimoksa texts

After building a large saṅgha the Buddha felt the need to develop a set of rules in various texts known as "Pratimoksa" that were recited many times. This included lifestyle choices, attire, and general ethics that they were to follow.

Around 508 BCE

Nirvana

Buddha dies at the age of 80 and leaves these final words,

All compounded things are ephemeral; work diligently on your salvation.
